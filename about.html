author

Minji Kim (b. 2000, Seoul, South Korea) (also known as magnolia and suk(i)) is an interdisciplinary artist working across poetic text, photography, and code-based media. Their practice examines the relationship between narrative and interface, focusing on how technology mediates intimacy, memory, and queer embodiment. Grounded in self-archiving and media archaeology, Kim’s research investigates underground publishing and DIY digital cultures as frameworks for contemporary queer world-making.
